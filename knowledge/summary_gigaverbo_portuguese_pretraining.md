# GigaVerbo: Portuguese Pretraining Corpus for Tucano Models

**Paper:** "Tucano: Advancing Neural Text Generation for Portuguese" (arXiv:2411.07854)
**Authors:** Nicholas Kluge Corrêa et al., Center for Science and Thought, University of Bonn
**Published:** Patterns (Cell Press), July 2025

## Dataset Overview

GigaVerbo is an extensive dataset comprising **780 GB** of Portuguese text, a concatenation of several openly available Portuguese text datasets, containing **over 200 billion tokens**. It was used to train the Tucano series of decoder-transformers natively pretrained in Portuguese.

The dataset has been:
- Deduplicated with an exact hash deduplication filter
- Filtered by `TucanoBR/BERTimbau-base-text-filter`

## The 9 Non-Synthetic Subsets (Used for Text-Filter Training)

The paper specifically selected **9 non-synthetic subsets** for building their text quality filter (`GigaVerbo-Text-Filter`). These are the subsets we target for our Portuguese pretraining mix:

1. **monoHPLT-PT** → maps to HPLT-PT subset
2. **CrawlPT** → maps to CC-2023 subset
3. **Wikipedia** → Wikipedia subset
4. **CulturaX** → CulturaX subset
5. **Common Crawl** → likely CCc100 or MC4-PT
6. **ROOTS** → Roots Wikiquote + Roots Ted Talks
7. **XL-Sum** → XL-Sum subset
8. **Corpus Carolina** → Corpus Carolina subset
9. **LegalPT** → Legal Portuguese subset

**Note:** The paper mentions that datasets like monoHPLT and Corpus Carolina have some of the lowest-quality samples. GPT-4o scoring reveals that samples containing toxic/harmful/NSFW content score < 0.1. Wikipedia samples that are ill-formatted, incomplete, or excessively short also score < 0.5.

## Quality Filtering via GigaVerbo-Text-Filter

- 110,000 randomly selected samples from the 9 non-synthetic subsets
- GPT-4o was used as a judge to score text quality (0-1 scale)
- Text embeddings generated by `sentence-transformers/LaBSE`
- Classifiers trained on these scored samples to filter GigaVerbo
- Available as `TucanoBR/GigaVerbo-Text-Filter` on HuggingFace
- The `label` field in GigaVerbo: 1 = high quality, 0 = low quality (both present in dataset)

## Relevance to Nanochat

### Data Sizing for 1B Parameter Model

From nanochat's scaling laws (discussion #420):
- **Optimal token-to-parameter ratio**: ~8:1 (with Muon optimizer), or ~10.5:1 (default target)
- For **1B parameters** at 10.5:1 ratio → **~10.5B tokens** needed
- For **1B parameters** at Chinchilla 20:1 → **~20B tokens** needed
- Karpathy's d34 (2.2B params) used 40:1 ratio → 88.7B tokens (intentional overtraining)

### ClimbMix-400B Reference

The current nanochat dataset (ClimbMix-400B) has:
- 6,542 shards of ~250M characters each
- ~100MB per shard (zstd compressed)
- Row group size: 1024 documents

### Mixing Strategy

To create a bilingual English+Portuguese pretraining mix:
- Use ClimbMix shards for English data
- Extract and repackage GigaVerbo non-synthetic subsets for Portuguese data
- Randomly interleave shards from both sources
- Match the shard format: parquet files, ~250M chars, row_group_size=1024, zstd compression
